What is Liquidity?

To understand what a liquidity sweep is, we first have to break down the concept of liquidity itself. You can find liquidity in every corner of the market — it sits right above and below every single candle. The simplest way to look at it is to view liquidity zones as the exact spots where traders place their stop losses. For instance, if a trader is in a winning long trade, they'll typically trail their stop loss just under the most recent swing low that price formed.

As you can see, a lot of people who entered long positions would most likely have their stop loss placed on that low created within price — so this is an area of liquidity.

What is a Liquidity Sweep?

What usually occurs after these areas of liquidity are created is a sweep of that liquidity. To identify a real liquidity sweep, you need to see a rejection within price after it has swept an area of liquidity.
