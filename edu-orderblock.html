What is an Order Block?

A high-probability Order Block (OB) requires a liquidity sweep to happen first. Once that's done, you mark the OB by highlighting the specific candle that took out that liquidity. Just like with an FVG, you expect price to return to this candle, tap it, and continue in its original direction.

Bullish OB Mark the last bearish candle that swept the liquidity.
Bearish OB Mark the last bullish candle that swept the liquidity.

The setup is ready once price closes above or below that OB candle — then you wait for price to trade back into it.

You can treat most candles as OBs if the orderflow is on your side — just make sure there was a liquidity sweep on some timeframe and a solid candle closure beyond the one that caused the sweep.
